Rangers and Hunter's Mark

Upon reaching your 2nd ranger level, you gain the following feature:

Hunter’s Mark

You know the spell Hunter’s Mark and may cast it once at first level without expending a spell slot. This spell does not count against the number of ranger spells you know. You regain the ability to cast Hunter’s Mark in this way when you complete a short or long rest, otherwise you must expend a spell slot to cast it again.
When you cast Hunter’s Mark, it does not require concentration.


Marked for Death

You have tasted death before, and it longs to greet you again. The more you resist, the more it continues to wear on you.

Marked for Death I

You have a -2 penalty to death saving throws. If you would gain one level of exhaustion, you gain an additional level of exhaustion.

Marked for Death II

You have a -5 penalty to death saving throws, replacing the penalty from Level I of this feature. Otherwise, you still suffer the effects of Level I.
Attack rolls made against you count as a critical hit on a roll of 19 or 20.

Marked for Death III

You have disadvantage on death saving throws, replacing the penalties from Level I & II of this feature. Otherwise, you still suffer the effects of Level I & II.
You are vulnerable to dmage from critical hits. Additionally, you are considered undead for the purposes of spells and abilities.
